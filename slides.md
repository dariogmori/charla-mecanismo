# permaculture
- creating sustainable practices for agriculture that replicate existing working relationships and systems found in nature
- avoids chemical pesticides or fertilizers in favor of other natural agricultural practices
- follows an approach that encourages regenerable and resilient processes based on existing systems found in nature

# permacomputing
## asks:
- is there a place for computers in a world where humans care for the biosphere?
- how can we obtain resilient and regenerative computing systems?
## suggests:
- adopting a sustainable approach that:
	- maximizes hardware lifespans
	- minimizes energy use
	- focuses on existing computing resources

# care for all hardware
especially the chips

- every device, chip and component comes from finite resources and end up as e-waste
- these devices take a huge part in the pollution generated by tech

- Value what you have
- Repair what can be repaired
- Think twice before buying new hardware 
	- and if you do buy second hand!

# optimize
your webpages/workloads

- compress images
- reduce javascript
- use lighter frameworks
- avoid unnecessary libraries

This reduces the energy needed to run/download your projects.
Makes it easier to run in lower-end hardware
---
layout: two-cols
layoutClass: gap-16
---
# regain
 your agency for creation

- Reduce the amount of agents you use for coding
- “optimize” your prompts
- Decrease your usage of third party AI models (or usage in general)
- learn something!
- Reduce your dependency on third party services (specially if they come with a subscription!)
---
layout: two-cols
layoutClass: gap-16
---
# think
about your reliance on technology
- what tech do you use on a daily basis? (devices, apps, credit card, transport... )
- what do those things provide you? 
- what would happen if you lost access to them? (lose your job, cannot buy food, cannot entertain yourself...)
